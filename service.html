What is Agrivoltaics?

This revolutionary technology will be an integral part of our sustainable
future, so here's a quick explanation!

The Basics

Put simply, Agrivoltaics is the placement of solar panels above crops.

The Science

The shade provided by the solar panels cools the air underneath.

At the same time, the plants' evapotranspiration increases ambient air moisture, which also reduces temperature.

The result is a microclimate effect!

The Benefits

Under reduced heat stress, shade-tolerant crops perform better.

Lower temperatures reduce evaporation, which both improves soil quality and reduces the amount of water needed to yield the same crop growth.

Plants act as natural air conditoners so solar can take advantage of sunlight without getting too hot to perform efficiently.

  • Drone Inspection

    Our quality assurance is driven by ML, AI, and drone technologies. Leveraging powerful aerial drones, we work with partner companies to cost-effectively detect damages to solar panels and replace them before they cause long-term energy losses.

  • Bifacial Modules

    In contrast to opaque back-sheeted panels, bifacial panels capture light both directly from the sun and from ground reflection.

    This additional absorbtion boosts energy production by up to 20% while only increasing module costs by 5%. And because cropland has a higher albedo than than bare ground, agrivoltaics augments the energy production of bi-facial modules.

  • Backtracking

    Traditonal, true tracking algorithms for mounted solar arrays treat each row independently. When each row operates without consideration of how the adjacent rows are oriented, solar panels shade each other.

    To avoid the losses associated with such shading, we implement a backtracking aglorithm that deviates the panels from true tracking in cases where such a deviation would increase energy yields.
